1. System Overview

The CPF Dashboard integrates two types of security assessments:

  • SOC Machine Data: Automated vulnerability detection from SIEM, IDS, EDR, and behavioral analytics
  • Field Kit Human Assessments: Manual audits performed by security professionals using structured questionnaires

These are merged using Bayesian inference to produce a single, confidence-weighted organizational risk score.

Two Dashboards

3. Bayesian Inference Explained

The dashboard merges SOC machine data and human auditor assessments using Bayesian inference.

How It Works

Step 1: Individual Indicator Merge

For each of the 100 indicators:

Bayesian Value = (SOC √ó confidence + Human √ó 1.5) / (confidence + 1.5)
  • SOC value: Machine-detected vulnerability (0-1)
  • Confidence: How certain the SOC is (0-1)
  • Human value: Auditor assessment (0-1)
  • 1.5x weight: Human assessments weighted higher (more trustworthy)

Example: Indicator 1.3 (Authority Deference)

  • SOC detects: 0.45 (confidence: 0.8)
  • Human audits: 0.65
  • Bayesian merge: (0.45 √ó 0.8 + 0.65 √ó 1.5) / (0.8 + 1.5) = 0.58

Interpretation: Human found higher risk than machine. Bayesian weighted toward human judgment.

Step 2: Cross-Category Dependencies

Some categories influence others. For example:

  • High Authority ‚Üí increases Social vulnerability (30%)
  • High Temporal ‚Üí increases Stress (40%)
  • High AI ‚Üí increases Cognitive load (30%)

The Bayesian engine adjusts category scores based on these dependencies.

Step 3: Overall Risk Calculation

Categories are weighted by importance:

Category Weight Rationale
Authority12%Most exploited in social engineering
AI12%Emerging threat vector
Social11%Trust-based attacks

Temporal10%Urgency manipulation

4. SOC vs Human Convergence

The convergence chart shows how machine and human assessments align over time.

Reading the Chart

  • Blue line: SOC machine data (daily aggregated)
  • Red line: Human auditor assessments (weekly)
  • X-axis: Time (30-day window)
  • Y-axis: Vulnerability score (0-100%)

What to Look For

‚úÖ Good: Close Convergence

SOC and Human lines are close (within 10%)

Meaning: Both detection methods agree. High confidence in assessment.

‚ö†Ô∏è Caution: Moderate Divergence

SOC and Human differ by 10-20%

Action: Review methodology. Human may detect nuances SOC misses (or vice versa).

üî¥ Alert: Large Divergence

SOC and Human differ by >20%

Action: Investigate immediately. Possible causes:

  • SOC misconfigured (false positives/negatives)
  • Human auditor bias
  • Rapid organizational change not captured by SOC
  • Cultural factors invisible to machine detection

Example: Divergence Analysis

Scenario: SOC shows 0.45, Human shows 0.75 (30% divergence)

Investigation:

  • Human detected cultural "trust executives blindly" (invisible to SOC)
  • SOC only sees technical metrics (email headers, timing)
  • Resolution: Trust human judgment. Add cultural training to remediation plan.

6. Prioritization Matrix

The prioritization table ranks categories by Priority Score:

Priority Score = (Risk √ó Weight) + Downstream Impact
  • Risk: Category vulnerability level (0-1)
  • Weight: Category importance (see Section 3)
  • Downstream Impact: How many other categories this influences

How to Use

  1. Sort by Priority Score (highest first)
  2. Focus on top 3 categories for remediation
  3. Fixing high-priority categories improves downstream categories automatically

Example: Prioritization Decision

Rank Category Risk Priority Score Downstream Impact
1 Temporal 0.82 0.28 0.95 (affects Stress, Affective, Cognitive)
2 Authority 0.75 0.24 0.65 (affects Social, Group)
3 Group 0.55 0.12 0.30

Action Plan:

  • Fix Temporal first ‚Üí Improves Stress, Affective, Cognitive automatically
  • Then Authority ‚Üí Improves Social and Group
  • Group will likely improve on its own (low priority)

9. Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Why is human weighted 1.5x vs SOC?

A: Human auditors detect cultural and behavioral nuances that machine learning cannot capture. Field research shows human assessments are more accurate for psychological vulnerabilities, hence higher weight.

Q: What if I only have SOC data (no human audits)?

A: The system still works. Bayesian will use SOC data only, but confidence will be lower. We recommend conducting at least quarterly human audits for critical organizations.

Q: What if I only have human audits (no SOC)?

A: Perfectly fine! Export Field Kit assessments and import into dashboard. Bayesian will use human-only data. This is actually common for smaller organizations without SOC infrastructure.

Q: How often should I re-assess?

A:

  • HIGH risk (0.67-1.00): Monthly
  • MEDIUM risk (0.34-0.66): Quarterly
  • LOW risk (0.00-0.33): Annually
  • After major organizational changes: Immediately

Q: Can I customize category weights?

A: Yes! Edit bayesian.js ‚Üí CATEGORY_WEIGHTS. For example, if your industry is heavily targeted by AI-based attacks, increase AI weight from 12% to 18%.

Q: What's "Conversation Completeness" in Field Kit?

A: It tracks how many open-ended conversation questions the auditor answered. This is informational only and does NOT affect the vulnerability score. It's for audit quality assurance.

Q: My SOC and Human scores diverge by 40%. What now?

A: Large divergence indicates a problem:

  1. Review SOC configuration (false positive rate?)
  2. Interview the human auditor (bias? methodology?)
  3. Conduct a third independent assessment
  4. Investigate recent organizational changes
Trust the data source with higher confidence. If both confidence is high, investigate why they disagree.

Q: What's the difference between CPF and phishing simulations?

A: Phishing sims test awareness post-training. CPF assesses organizational culture and psychological vulnerabilities that enable attacks. CPF is predictive; phishing sims are reactive. Use both!

Q: Is this scientifically validated?

A: The CPF framework is based on established psychological research (authority bias, temporal pressure, etc.). The Bayesian dependency matrix is empirically derived but continuously refined. For academic validation, see the research paper (coming soon).
